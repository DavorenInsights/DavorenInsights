Published as architecture and artifact logic. Built for constrained, multi-stakeholder execution environments.

What it clarifies Structural condition

What it clarifies

The decision questions the instrument forces into structure.

  • Readiness: What must be evidenced for this initiative to be commit-ready — now?
  • Constraints: Which bottlenecks are structural, and which are addressable through sequencing?
  • Execution exposure: Where does failure appear first — evidence, coordination, or capacity?
  • Decision posture: Should the correct move be commit, pause, re-sequence, or re-scope?

Structural condition

What changed, and why this instrument exists.

2–4 sentences. State the structural shift. Name the constraint or incentive. Describe what becomes ambiguous without an instrument like this.

1–2 sentences. State the late-stage failure mode this instrument is designed to prevent. Keep it concrete (deal collapse, procurement stall, partner withdrawal, audit failure).

Inputs and outputs

Interface-level description only.

Inputs

Input categories only (no internal mechanism): evidence types, context assumptions, constraint context, stakeholder landscape, governance requirements, time horizon.

Outputs

Output artifact types only: summaries, registers, exposure maps, sequencing notes, decision memos. Describe how to interpret them (exposure markers, not certifications).

Interpretation rules

How outputs should be read without over-claiming validity.

2–4 sentences. Clarify what “high readiness” means in this instrument (artifact density, coherence, explicit sequencing), and what it does not mean (certainty of success).

1–2 sentences. State the decision shift the instrument enforces (from preference to structure, from acceleration to composition).

Validity conditions

When this instrument is valid — and when it is not.

  • Valid when: a decision window is approaching and artifact coherence is required.
  • Invalid when: used as a certification, marketing signal, or substitute for due diligence.
  • Depends on: input recency, evidence integrity, and declared constraints (suppressed risks invalidate outputs).
  • Primary failure mode prevented: commitments made on narrative, collapsing under scrutiny.
