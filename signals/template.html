Signal 001 Feb 2026 • 3–5 min

Signal Title

One sentence: what shifted, in structural terms.

Innovation Futures Decision

Shift

One sentence: what changed in the system.

Driver

The constraint/incentive that is producing the shift.

Indicator

One thing to watch that confirms (or falsifies) the signal.

Exposure

Who benefits, who breaks, what decision now looks different.

Observation

State what is happening. Concrete. Minimal interpretation. If it’s an observation you made (not “new”), say so plainly: “This is not new; what’s new is the intensity/placement/timing.”

Mechanism

Name the causal structure: constraint, incentives, coordination, adoption friction. Keep it mechanistic. No advocacy. No hype.

  • What constraint is tightening?
  • What incentive moved?
  • Where does coordination fail?
  • What assumption stops holding?

Structural implication

What this changes across the system. Not “tips.” Consequences: capital flow, adoption rates, execution risk, institutional load.

Strategic note

One or two lines. Compression. No theatrics. Example: Coordination debt compounds quietly before it appears in metrics.
